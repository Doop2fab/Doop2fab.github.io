A bit about me

Ever since I was a kid, I'd always wanted to know exactly how the things around me worked, no matter how complex and incomprehensible it was to an 8 year-old. At school, I was borderline obsessed with the computers there, always making a point to talk about it to my parents.

For a couple Christmases, a computer was at the very top of my list. I vividly recall dreaming about what endless possibilities there were just waiting for me behind the computer screen. Eventually, I was given a "tip-off" by my parents that Santa was going to bring us our very own computer.

As soon as I woke up, I raced down the stairs to see if it was true... and it was. As soon as my dad set up our Dell Desktop, I was asking questions like, "How does it work?", "What can I do with it?", and "When is it my turn?". That Christmas, my dad got me my first ever full-fledged computer game to go along with it - SPORE.

The creativity and curiosity that SPORE helped draw out of me still burns brightly to this day. No matter where I am or what I'm doing, I ask those same three questions 8 year-old me was so fond of.

I realize I have plenty of learning ahead of me, and what I learn will constantly be changing. Well I say bring on change, because my curiosity will never be quenched and, quite frankly, I wouldn't have it any other way.
